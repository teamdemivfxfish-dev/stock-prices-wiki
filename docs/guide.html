Stock Prices - Supply and Demand

A living, self-balancing economy for your server.

NeoForge 1.21.1  |  modid stockprices  |  v1.1.0  |  by Barunn (NewtL)  |  soft-deps: Spud's Shop, SDM Economy

Live on CurseForge

Stock Prices is not a shop mod; it is the price engine behind one. Every obtainable item is given a Standard Retail Price (SRP) that is recomputed once per Minecraft day from the live state of the world: how much of it exists (supply), how concentrated it is into a few hands (hoarding), how much money is circulating (inflation), and how much was bought lately (demand). Prices rise when things get scarce, hoarded, or when money floods in, and fall when the world is awash in supply. Players move the market by mining, farming, hoarding in vaults, flooding a good, or cutting off a supply line.

Press the keybind to open the Market Prices screen and read any item's true value with a 7-day trend arrow. With Spud's Shop installed, a per-stall checkbox hands a stall's price over to the market. With SDM Economy installed, every account balance is totalled into the money pool so bank wealth feeds inflation.

Everything is relative to opening day. On the first recompute the engine calibrates its supply and money references so day-one prices equal the base prices; you see movement only after you change the world from that baseline. One easing rate governs every force, so a rise and a crash behave identically: a big shock still resolves within a day or two.

The economy model

Every Minecraft day, for each commodity, the engine computes a target price from four forces and eases the live price toward it at a single rate:

H = supply / occupiedLocations effSupply = supply / (1 + (H / Hc)^m) target = base * demandFactor(purchases) * (Sref / effSupply)^kS * (money / Mref)^kM price += (target - price) * adjustSpeed

supply / occupiedLocations = average per location (hoarding)  •  effSupply = circulating supply after piles discount out  •  Sref, Mref auto-calibrate on first run

  • Supply pulls the price down; scarcity pushes it up when effective supply is low.
  • Hoarding is folded into effSupply: a stockpile far above the per-location threshold discounts itself out of circulation, so the price climbs even when the raw total is high. A monopolist dumping their vault causes a hard crash; the same total spread evenly barely moves.
  • Money supply inflates every price; more money pooled across the world pushes prices up.
  • Demand (buying pressure) pushes prices up. Real purchase tracking needs Spud's Shop; until purchases are seen the demand factor stays neutral.
  • Zero supply clamps at the ceiling; all prices are held within priceFloor and priceCeil.

Supply scanning

The mod keeps its own per-chunk index of every item in container inventories via the item-handler capability. Online players' inventories and ender chests count as distinct locations for the hoarding math (configurable). Scanning is throttled: loaded chunks are queued and only scanBudgetPerTick chunks are scanned per server tick, so joining a world never stalls the load screen. Only chunks that actually contain containers are queued. At the day rollover the loaded chunks are re-scanned before pricing, and the recompute is held until the post-join catch-up scan has fully settled, so a half-built index can never corrupt good prices.

Money supply (inflation)

The money pool is the sum of two sources. Physical currency: every currency item in the world, counted at its [currency] denominations value, so hoarding or printing currency inflates every price. SDM Economy balances (optional): with SDM installed and sdmEnabled on, every account balance is totalled in and added on top; which currencies count and what each is worth is set by sdmCurrencies, and locked balances are skipped.

Commodities & bills

A commodity is any obtainable item (everything except air, spawn eggs, and a technical blacklist of un-obtainable blocks). Items can be grouped so reversible packing and cross-mod synonyms price as one thing (an iron block is 9 ingots), while one-way processing (ore vs ingot) is kept separate so a smelting bottleneck shows up. The screen shows one honest base-unit number, but when a price is written onto a Spud's stall it is expressed as the fewest of the largest denomination that fits: 1000 becomes 10 diamonds (diamond = 100), not 1000 emeralds.

Compatibility

All integrations are optional soft dependencies, guarded by a runtime ModList.isLoaded check and never bundled.

  • Spud's Shop (spudaciousshops): a checkbox in the stall owner screen toggles that stall to SRP mode; when on, the daily recompute writes the computed price (as denominated bills) onto the stall. Toggle state persists.
  • SDM Economy (sdmeconomy): account balances are totalled into the money pool, feeding inflation. Uses SDM's public economy API; on any error it falls back to a neutral value.
  • SDM Shop (sdmshop): listed as an optional dependency in the mod metadata.
